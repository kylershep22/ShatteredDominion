**Design Pillars:**

- Power jumps should feel like power jumps — not margin gains  
- Crafted items and found items occupy separate power bands — neither invalidates the other  
- The socket/Combination system is its own progression track, completely separate from standard item tiers  
- Every item tier above Common requires a decision, not just an upgrade  
- Materials are grounded in the world — iron comes from the earth, leather from animals, timber from forests  
- The world's lore lives in the loot

# SECTION 1: DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

## 1.1 Two Parallel Progression Tracks

The loot system operates on two completely independent tracks that never directly compete:

**Track 1 — The Finder's Track:** Discovering and equipping Touched, Forged, Bound, and Remnant items. Power comes from affix combinations, set bonuses, and named item properties. This track rewards exploration, combat, and faction investment.

**Track 2 — The Builder's Track:** Socketing Relic Shards into Common items to activate Shard Combinations. Power comes from the Combination's named effect. This track rewards material gathering, Shard hunting, and knowledge of which Combinations exist.

**These tracks do not intersect at the item level.** Shard Combinations only activate in Common items. Touched, Forged, Bound, and Remnant items have no sockets and cannot be used for Combinations. A player wearing a Remnant sword is making a different choice than a player wielding a 6-socket Common sword tuned for The Void Spiral Combination. Both choices are valid. Neither is strictly better — they offer different things.

This separation is the most important structural decision in this document. It prevents power stacking (Combination effects on top of Forged affixes), keeps both systems readable, and ensures Common items are never truly trash.

## 1.2 Crafted vs. Found — A Separate Power Band

Crafted items and found items do not compete directly. They offer fundamentally different things:

- **Found items (Touched through Remnant):** Variable affix combinations, named Remnant properties, set bonuses. Power comes from what you find and how lucky or persistent you are.  
- **Crafted items:** Guaranteed affix combinations, craftsman-specific properties unavailable on any drop, and quality ceilings tied to crafting skill investment. Power comes from what you build and how skilled you've become.

A master blacksmith can forge a sword with perfectly controlled affix pairing that no drop can replicate. But that sword will never have a Remnant's unique named property or a set bonus. The smith's sword is reliable, customized, and powerful within its band. The Remnant is singular, fixed, and irreplaceable. Players who invest in both find they complement each other — a crafted belt with perfectly chosen affixes alongside a Remnant amulet represents the full system working as intended.

## 1.3 Drop Feel — Moderate Density, Curated Quality

Common items drop freely. They are ambient world loot — expected background noise that occasionally produces a socketed item worth examining.

Touched items drop from standard combat. Finding one is unremarkable but welcome.

Forged items drop from meaningful encounters. A well-rolled Forged is a small event.

Bound pieces, Remnants, and high-socket Common items are specific content rewards — bosses, dungeon completions, faction quest payoffs. These drops are always meaningful. No exceptions.

**Identification:** All Touched tier and above drop unidentified. Identification requires a scroll (common consumable) or a city's General Workbench (free, always available). This creates the D2 anticipation loop without punishing players who don't have scrolls.

# SECTION 2 REVISION: QUALITY TIERS & SOCKET RULES

## Updated Socket Architecture

The socket system has two distinct purposes that operate independently:

**Purpose 1 — Shard Combinations (Builder's Track):** Only Common items. Up to 6 sockets. Combinations require all sockets filled with specific Shards. This is the runeword-equivalent system. Unchanged.

**Purpose 2 — Individual Shard Bonuses (Supplemental Enhancement):** Touched, Forged, and Bound items can roll up to 3 sockets. These sockets hold individual Shards for their standalone bonuses only — no Combinations activate in non-Common items, regardless of how many Shards are socketed or which types. The bonus is purely additive: a Forge Shard in a Forged sword's socket gives its \+Physical damage and \+Armor penetration bonus, nothing more.

**Remnant items** do not roll sockets randomly. However, specific Remnant items may have 1–2 sockets written into their fixed profile as part of their identity. When a Remnant has sockets, they function identically to sockets on Touched/Forged items — individual Shard bonuses only, no Combinations.

## 3.2 Armor Weight Classes

Weight class is a **soft trade-off system**. Any character can equip any armor regardless of stats. The trade-offs are innate to the armor type, not gated behind stat requirements. Weapons retain stat requirements (see Section 4).

Mixed weight class loadouts are fully supported — a player wearing a Heavy chest with Light legs and Medium gloves experiences a blended trade-off profile calculated from the weighted average of equipped pieces.

### Weight Class Trade-Off Table

| Weight Class | Armor Rating | Cast Speed | Stamina Regen | Movement Speed | Dodge Window |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Heavy** | Highest (+40% base) | −20% | −25% | −10% | Narrower |
| **Medium** | Moderate (+15% base) | −5% | −10% | Neutral | Standard |
| **Light** | Lowest (base) | \+10% | \+10% | \+5% | Wider |
| **Unarmored** | None | \+20% | \+20% | \+10% | Widest |

**Unarmored** is a valid build choice — Aetherborn and stealth-focused builds can opt for no armor in specific slots, gaining maximum mobility and cast speed at the cost of all protection. Affixes and Shard Combinations are the primary defense for these builds.

## 3.3 The Relic Slot

The Relic Slot is permanent and unique. The player's bonded relic cannot be removed. It begins with 1 socket and expands to a maximum of 6 through story progression milestones. Shards socketed here interact with Echo abilities and lattice-based skills specifically. Relic Slot Combinations are a separate catalogue from standard gear Combinations (see Section 5.5).

# SECTION 4 REVISION: EXPANDED WEAPON CATALOG

## Design Philosophy for Weapon Depth

Each weapon *category* now contains multiple distinct weapon *types* that share a general combat identity but differ in speed, damage profile, reach, and special properties. This creates genuine horizontal progression — a player building a one-handed sword build has multiple swords to compare, each with different strengths, rather than simply finding the same sword at a higher level.

Weapons are organized into **base categories** (which govern proficiency tracks) and **subtypes** within each category (which have individual stat profiles). Proficiency in a base category applies to all subtypes within it — a player with high Sword proficiency handles both Short Swords and Scimitars well.

# SECTION 5 REVISION: EXPANDED ARMOR CATALOG

## Design Philosophy for Armor Depth

Each armor slot now contains multiple named options within each weight class. These are not just "the same armor at a higher level" — each named piece has a distinct construction that lends itself to different affix pools, different aesthetic identities, and different trade-off expressions within its weight class. Heavier construction within a weight class provides more armor rating but less mobility. Players progression through choices, not just upgrades.

Armor pieces are organized by **slot**, then **weight class**, then **named types** within that class. The types exist at varying points in the progression — some are available early, some require faction access or crafting investment.

# SECTION 6: THE RELIC SHARD SYSTEM

## 6.1 Core Architecture

Relic Shards are fragments of Firstborn lattice infrastructure — broken loose by the Severance and scattered across the world. They are socketed exclusively into Common items. When specific Shards are combined in a single Common item, they produce a named Combination effect that surpasses the sum of their individual bonuses — the game's runeword-equivalent system.

**Shard Combinations are the Builder's Track endgame.** A 6-socket Common sword with The Void Spiral Combination is not a "lesser" item than a Remnant sword — it is a different item with a different power identity. The Combination enables a HP-as-mana playstyle that no Remnant can replicate. The Remnant offers a fixed, named identity that no Combination can replicate. Both are endgame choices.

## 6.2 Shard Types (9 Types)

| Shard | Symbol | Individual Socket Effect | Lore Origin |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Anchor Shard** | ⚓ | \+Armor rating, \+Crowd-control resistance | Deep structural lattice conduits |
| **Flow Shard** | ≋ | \+Mana regeneration, \+Cast speed | Mana-flow conduit network |
| **Pulse Shard** | ◎ | \+Attack speed, \+On-hit proc chance | Lattice energy pulse generators |
| **Ward Shard** | ⬡ | \+Elemental resistance, \+HP regeneration | Lattice defense nodes |
| **Fracture Shard** | ✦ | \+Critical strike chance, \+Shadow damage | Damaged/corrupted lattice segments |
| **Verdant Shard** | ❧ | \+Nature damage, \+Healing received | Wellspring-adjacent conduits |
| **Archive Shard** | ⊕ | \+1 to ability tree, \+Intelligence | Firstborn data storage nodes |
| **Forge Shard** | ⚙ | \+Physical damage, \+Armor penetration | Firstborn manufacturing nodes |
| **Void Shard** | ◌ | \+Mana pool, \+Spell damage at cost of HP | Sealed void segments — dangerous |

## 6.3 Combination Rules

- A Common item must have exactly the required socket count filled to activate a Combination  
- Additional filled sockets beyond the Combination requirement add their individual bonuses on top  
- Combinations are order-independent — the Echo reads the full configuration  
- One named Combination activates per item (the most powerful qualifying pattern takes precedence)  
- Combinations are discovered through: Combination Tablets found in Firstborn ruins, Hollow Crown archive research, and specific quest rewards

# SECTION 9: CRAFTING SYSTEM

## 9.1 Design Philosophy

Crafting is a deep, parallel progression path. A player who invests heavily in Blacksmithing builds a different kind of powerful character than one who focuses on finding loot — and neither is wrong. The crafting system is grounded in physical reality: iron must be mined, smelted, and forged. Hides must be harvested, cured, and stitched. Herbs must be gathered, dried, and combined. The world's post-Severance scarcity makes quality materials feel genuinely valuable.

Crafted items occupy their own power band. They do not try to be Remnants. Instead, they offer **guaranteed affix combinations** — the player chooses exactly what goes on the item — and **craftsman properties** that appear only on crafted gear, unavailable on any drop.

## 9.2 Crafting Disciplines

Six independent crafting skill trees. Each levels separately. No shared pool — a player can max every discipline if they invest the time, but doing so in a single playthrough requires significant dedication. Most players will max 1–2 disciplines and leave others at functional mid-levels.

Each discipline has **10 skill tiers**. Advancing requires crafting items of sufficient complexity at the current tier — simply making the same basic item repeatedly yields diminishing returns past a threshold.

### BLACKSMITHING

*Weapons and Heavy Armor*

**Governs:** All metal weapons (swords, axes, blunt, polearms, crossbows), Heavy armor pieces, shields. **Primary Stats:** Scales with Strength and Endurance — higher stats improve base damage output and armor rating of crafted items.

| Tier | Unlocks |
| :---- | :---- |
| 1–2 | Iron-quality gear (Common-equivalent), basic repairs |
| 3–4 | Steel-quality gear, basic affix control (choose 1 affix from a limited list) |
| 5–6 | High-carbon steel gear, 2 controlled affixes, access to socketed base crafting (Common items with guaranteed sockets) |
| 7–8 | Alloy gear (Silverite, Ashsteel), 3 controlled affixes, craftsman properties unlock |
| 9–10 | Legendary material work, 4 controlled affixes, masterwork items with craftsman-exclusive properties |

**Craftsman Properties (Blacksmithing — appear only on crafted gear):**

- *Ironkin's Balance:* Weapon weight perfectly distributed — no stamina cost on the first 3 attacks of each combat  
- *Tempered Edge:* Blade holds its edge — bleed effects applied by this weapon deal damage 40% longer  
- *Fortress Frame:* Armor crafted with this property adds a secondary HP pool (15% of max HP) that regenerates between combats  
- *Battle-Tested:* Item gains \+2% to all its affixes per Act completed while equipped (accumulates permanently)

### Player Stronghold

The player's stronghold (unlocked through the main story, Act II) can be upgraded with personal crafting stations. Upgrading a station in the stronghold provides a permanent bonus to that discipline's output (+5% quality) and removes the need to travel to city stations for standard work.

Stronghold crafting station upgrades are a meaningful economic investment (see Faction Playbook, Section 6 — Economic System).

# SECTION 10: MATERIALS

## 10.1 Material System Philosophy

Materials are grounded in the physical world. Players gather raw materials through exploration, mining, harvesting, hunting, and trade. Raw materials must be refined before use in crafting — one step across all material types (Skyrim-faithful), but the ratio between raw input and refined output varies by material quality.

**Refinement Ratio Rule:**

- Common-tier raw materials: 5 raw → 1 refined  
- Uncommon-tier raw materials: 3 raw → 1 refined  
- Rare-tier raw materials: 2 raw → 1 refined  
- Legendary-tier raw materials: 1 raw → 1 refined (the material is already close to ideal; refinement is a processing step, not a conversion step)

Refinement happens at the appropriate station (smelting at the Blacksmith, curing at the Leatherworker, brewing at the Apothecary). It is not automated — the player must initiate refinement. This is a light friction that makes material management feel intentional without being punishing.

## 10.3 Material Gathering

**Mining:** Iron ore, coal iron, Highwinter iron, Void ore, Latticite — gathered at deposit nodes throughout the world. Nodes respawn on a real-time cycle. Higher-tier deposits require the player to be in appropriate areas (Void ore only in corruption zones, Highwinter iron only from Vanguard trade shipments or northern expeditions).

**Harvesting:** Timber, Wellspring Timber, Eldertimber — gathered at tree nodes. Logging axe required (tool item). Ironbark requires a reinforced axe (Uncommon tool).

**Hunting:** Animal hides — dropped by wildlife. Beast quantity decreases post-Severance (noted in the world state). Ancient beast hides from Act II+ enemy types.

**Herbalism:** All herb varieties — gathered in the field. Wellspring herbs found only in Verdantheart-adjacent zones. Rare alchemical reagents from specific biomes.

**Salvaging:** Breaking down found items (any tier) yields a small amount of their primary material component. A Common iron sword salvaged at a Blacksmith yields 1–2 Iron Ore equivalent. A Remnant item salvaged yields a small amount of Rare material. Salvaging is the last resort — it recovers less than the item is worth sold, but provides materials you specifically need.

**Purchasing from Merchants:** Common and Uncommon materials are available from relevant merchants (general stores, the Verdant Flame apothecary, the Vanguard quartermaster). Rare materials are occasionally available through faction merchant networks at high prices. Legendary materials are never sold — they must be found.

# SECTION 11: DROP SOURCES & LOOT DESIGN

## 11.1 Drop Philosophy

Common items drop freely. Touched items from meaningful combat. Forged items from significant encounters. Bound pieces from specific content. Remnants from bosses, dungeon completions, and faction rewards.

**Guaranteed drops are non-negotiable:** Every boss drops at least one Remnant or Bound piece. Every dungeon completion chest contains at least two Forged items. Players are never left empty-handed after investing significant effort.

**Contextual theming:** Drop pools are biased by location. Vanguard dungeons drop Vanguard-tendency gear. Corruption zones drop Shadow and Fracture affixed Forged items more frequently. Hidden rooms reward exploration with Forged and occasionally Remnant items.

## 11.2 Drop Source Table

| Source | Expected Output |
| :---- | :---- |
| Standard enemies | Common, occasional Touched |
| Elite enemies (named) | Touched, Forged |
| Dungeon midpoint chest | Guaranteed 1 Forged, area-themed |
| Dungeon completion chest | 2–3 Forged \+ Bound piece or Remnant |
| Boss | 2–3 Forged \+ guaranteed Remnant or Bound |
| Faction quest completion | Faction-appropriate Forged or Bound piece |
| Hidden room | Forged, occasional Remnant |
| High-socket Common (4–6) | Found in dungeon chests and boss drops as alternatives to Touched/Forged |
| Crafting | Touched through Custom Remnant (discipline-dependent) |
