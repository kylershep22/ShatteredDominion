# SECTION 1: REGIONAL OVERVIEW

## 1.1 The Vale at a Glance

The Severant Vale was, until four weeks ago, the continent's breadbasket — a fertile, temperate region of rolling farmland, mixed forest, and riverside settlements that fed the Dominion and connected its trade routes. It was unremarkable in the way that productive, stable places are unremarkable. People built things. Families stayed for generations. The lattice hummed beneath the soil and nobody thought about it.

The Severance changed everything in a single night. Infrastructure that had been quietly running civilization for four centuries failed simultaneously. The crops that survived are growing wrong. The Firstborn ruins dotting the landscape — ignored, built around, occasionally looted — are now active, hostile, and leaking.

The Vale is not a post-apocalyptic wasteland. It is a place in acute crisis: past the initial shock, not yet into long-term adaptation. People are scared, angry, grieving, and improvising. The world they knew is gone. The world that replaces it hasn't been decided yet. That decision is, in part, what the player is here to make.

## 1.2 Geography

**Size:** 40–45 km² playable area. This is a deliberate design decision. Player perception of world size is driven by discovery density, not raw terrain. A 40–45 km² map with high authored content density will feel larger than a 120 km² map with sparse content because something meaningful happens every few minutes of travel. Every km² earns its place.

**Elevation range:** 50m (river valley, Hollowford) to 400m (eastern ridge approaching Verdantheart).

**Biome:** Temperate mixed forest and farmland. Four seasons, though post-Severance weather has become unpredictable — arcane storms, sudden frosts, heat waves with no meteorological cause. Sky occasionally flickers with aurora effects from lingering lattice energy.

**Primary waterway:** River Serpentis, flowing southwest from the northeastern hills through the Vale's center. Clean, reliable. One of the few things the Severance didn't break.

**Quadrant framework:**

| Quadrant | Primary Features | Dominant Faction Presence |
|---|---|---|
| **NW — Highlands Border & Ironwood** | Dense forest, logging trails, Hollow Crown approach | Hollow Crown agents, Vanguard patrols |
| **NE — Verdantheart Border & Grainfell** | Farmland, river tributary, corruption zone creep | Verdant Flame scouts, corrupted wildlife |
| **SW — Hollowford & River Valley** | City, roads, cleared farmland, River Serpentis | All factions, Vanguard dominant |
| **SE — Crimson Flats Border & Southwatch** | Drier terrain, heat haze, refugee camps | Drakeling refugees, minimal Vanguard |

## 1.3 The Region's Three States

The Vale exists in three states that shift across Acts and player choices.

**Crisis State (Act I):** Factions establishing positions. Corruption zones new and spreading. Refugees arriving. Everything unstable.

**Conflict State (Act II — automatic on Ashenveil revelation):** Faction tensions overt. Corruption expands. Hollowford shows faction control markers. Player choices begin having visible consequences.

**Resolution State (Act III/IV — branching by alignment):** The Vale looks different depending on the player's Act III alignment. Full details in Section 8.

# SECTION 2: FAST TRAVEL SYSTEM

## 2.1 Hub-and-Spoke Model

Fast travel is restricted to **12 major hub nodes**. These hubs unlock automatically when the player first arrives at each location — no discovery action required. Everything between hubs requires physical travel.

**The 12 fast travel hubs:**
1. Hollowford — North Gate
2. Hollowford — South Gate
3. Hollowford — East Gate
4. Hollowford — West Gate
5. Ironwood (settlement center)
6. Grainfell (settlement center)
7. Ashford (settlement center)
8. Southwatch (settlement center)
9. The Sepulcher (dungeon entrance — unlocks after first visit)
10. Verdant Temple (dungeon entrance — unlocks after first visit)
11. Sunken Hollow (dungeon entrance — unlocks after first visit)
12. Ashenveil Archive (dungeon entrance — unlocks after first visit)

**What does NOT fast travel:** Named wilderness landmarks, minor ruins, refugee camps, faction outposts, corruption zones, points of interest. These require physical travel. This is the gap the faction travel systems fill.

## 2.2 Fast Travel Restrictions

Unavailable during: active combat, over-encumbrance, specific story-locked moments (clearly communicated before locking).

## 2.3 Faction Travel — The Premium Mobility Layer

Faction travel handles everything fast travel cannot. The three systems are not redundant with fast travel — they are the infrastructure that makes the world between hubs navigable and meaningful.

**Vanguard Road Network:**
Maintained roads between all four hub settlements and Hollowford. Traveling by road (not fast travel — actual overland movement) at Auxiliary standing or above provides: Vanguard escort reducing random encounter chance to near-zero, guaranteed checkpoint passage without interrogation, and +30% overland travel speed on maintained road surfaces. For players who invested in the Vanguard, the road is genuinely faster and safer than cross-country movement. The roads are also the only reliable path to the frontier settlements in heavy rain or winter weather, when cross-country terrain becomes difficult. Making the Vanguard connection means the roads work for you. Opposing the Vanguard means traveling around them.

**Reaver Tunnel Network:**
Underground passages connecting Hollowford's Undercity to specific *non-hub* locations fast travel cannot reach: hidden cache sites, dungeon back entrances, faction outpost locations that Vanguard doesn't know about. Unlocked at Crew-Adjacent standing. The tunnels make Reaver investment genuinely valuable for access, not just economy. A Reaver-aligned player can reach the back entrance of the Sunken Hollow Vault and skip the first combat section entirely. A player who never invested in the Reavers has to find the front door. The difference is felt.

**Verdant Flame Waypoints:**
Natural meditation points scattered across forested and wilderness areas. At Circle Ally standing, the Verdant Flame teaches the player to move through these waypoints at +50% travel speed. In a world where you are physically walking between hubs, this is meaningful — cutting through the Corruption Front at Verdant Flame waypoint speed versus fighting through it on foot is a different experience. Waypoints also partially restore stamina and mana when used, making them rest points as well as transit nodes.

# SECTION 3: AUTHORED MICRO-DISCOVERIES

## 3.1 Design Philosophy

Micro-discoveries are authored environmental storytelling scenes that exist purely to reward players who explore off the path. They do not appear in the quest journal. They have no markers. They are not flagged as discoveries. They are simply things that exist in the world and that players find or don't find.

**Target: 60–80 across the entire Vale.**

Each micro-discovery costs a fraction of a formal quest's resource investment. Together they produce a map where something authored can happen at any moment of exploration — where veering off the road might produce a window into someone's last night, or a Firstborn observation that recontextualizes something the player thought they understood, or a moment that is simply strange and beautiful and unexplained.

The cumulative effect: a world that feels inhabited and layered rather than constructed. Players who explore thoroughly find a different Vale than players who follow the main path. Both are valid. Neither is the "real" version.

## 3.2 Micro-Discovery Categories and Examples

**Personal remnants** — Evidence of specific people who lived here:
- A farmhouse where a family clearly left in a hurry. One person didn't make it to the door. Their journal is on the table. Three paragraphs. No quest marker, no resolution, just a window into someone's last night. The entry from the night of the Severance is incomplete — the sentence stops mid-word.
- A child's rope swing in a tree at the edge of a corrupted field. The tree is wrong now but the swing is untouched. Someone tied it there. They may still be alive somewhere.
- A Vanguard patrol that set up camp and hasn't moved in three days. Getting close reveals why. Not every discovery is a mystery with an answer — some are just what happened.
- Two graves marked with Dominion soldier insignia, recently dug. A third patch of disturbed earth beside them. The third grave is empty.

**Firstborn traces** — Evidence of what was here before:
- A Firstborn survey marker embedded in a hillside with a lattice energy signature. Using Echo Interface produces a 10-second fragment of what the Firstborn were measuring here and why. The measurement was anomalous. The Firstborn noted it and continued.
- A perfectly preserved Firstborn measuring device embedded in a riverbank — still functional, still measuring water flow, displaying readings in a unit system the Echo recognizes but that no living person uses.
- A circle of stones that turns out to be the exposed top of a buried Firstborn structure. There is no entrance. There is no quest to find one. It's just there, intact, sealed, waiting.
- A location where the ground produces a faint harmonic tone when walked over. Aelira, if in the party, pauses and says something. It's the sound a functioning lattice node makes. There's nothing beneath it anymore — the sound is a memory in the stone.

**Faction footprints** — Evidence of faction activity before the player arrives:
- A Reaver dead drop that was clearly compromised — the hidden container is empty and someone left a calling card. Not the Vanguard's calling card.
- A Verdant Flame purification attempt at a corruption zone edge. It didn't work. The practitioner left their tools behind and the tools are still warm.
- A Hollow Crown survey flag stuck in a hillside near Ashford. On the back, in small handwriting: a geological notation that implies the survey predated the Severance by two years. They knew.
- A Vanguard requisition notice nailed to the door of an abandoned farmhouse — polite, official language — demanding grain delivery to a garrison that no longer exists.

**Vaelari and Drakeling fragments** — Evidence of displaced peoples:
- A lone Vaelari woman at the edge of a corruption zone, attempting a purification ritual that is clearly not working. She doesn't want help. She doesn't want to talk. She is there every time the player passes, in Act I and Act II. In Act III, if the Verdant Flame alignment was chosen, she is not there anymore — the zone is smaller and she has gone somewhere else. If not, she is still there and the ritual is still failing.
- A small Drakeling shrine built from whatever materials were available — clearly temporary, clearly maintained daily. The offering at its base is fresh. Someone is keeping this.
- A Newly Awakened Vaelari sitting on a fallen log holding a piece of paper they cannot read. They have been holding it since they woke. It is a letter addressed to someone they were, in a world they were never part of.

**World-state anomalies** — Things that shouldn't be:
- A tree that is half-corrupted, the corruption stopping exactly at the midline of the trunk. The non-corrupted half is flowering out of season.
- A pond where the reflection shows the sky as it was before the Severance — clear, no aurora flicker. The actual sky above is wrong. The water is correct.
- A section of road where all sound stops. Not quiet — silent. Footsteps, wind, birds, all absent. The silence ends exactly at the road's edge. Nothing explains it. The Echo does not react.

## 3.3 Micro-Discovery Distribution

Micro-discoveries are distributed unevenly — deliberately. They cluster in:
- Areas adjacent to corruption zones (what the corruption is doing to the world)
- Areas between hub locations (reward for overland travel)
- Areas visible from the road but requiring a short detour (reward for curiosity)
- Deep wilderness areas (reward for significant exploration)

They thin out inside Hollowford (where authored NPC content is dense enough) and inside settlements (where formal quest hooks serve the same discovery function).

Level designers are responsible for ensuring no two micro-discoveries are visible from each other. Density should be felt cumulatively across hours of play, not experienced as a theme park of encounter markers.

# SECTION 4: HOLLOWFORD — THE LAST CITY

## 4.1 Overview

**Population:** ~12,000 (pre-Severance: ~8,000; refugee influx adds ~4,000)
**Size:** ~1.2 km² walled city + surrounding refugee camps
**Controlling faction:** Ironbound Vanguard (martial law, contested)

Hollowford was never supposed to matter this much. It was a regional hub — a ford crossing, a market, a waypoint. Now it is the last functioning city in the Vale. Every faction has representatives here. Every displaced person is trying to get here, or leave, or survive within its walls.

## 4.2 NPC Schedules — Crossing District Lines

Every major named NPC has a daily schedule that takes them outside their home district at least once per in-game day. These schedules cost almost nothing to implement and make the city feel inhabited rather than staged. Players who pay attention understand who people are before they ever take a quest from them.

**Schedule examples:**
- **Harren Voss** (Merchant, Market Quarter): Walks to the Garrison quartermaster every morning to negotiate supply contracts. Returns midday. Visits the Broken Chalice at dusk for one drink he never finishes.
- **Lady Mirelle Ashford** (Noble Quarter): Visits the Healer's Sanctuary twice a week — she is funding it quietly and does not want it known. Her route takes her through the Market Quarter and she walks quickly, hood up.
- **Torven Ashpeak** (Forge District): Comes to the Broken Chalice on the seventh day of each in-game week. He has not missed this in thirty years. He sits alone. He does not seek conversation but will accept it.
- **Kael Thornwright** (Garrison): Walks the Market Quarter alone at dusk. This is the only time he can think. He is off-duty, unarmed in the visible sense, and pays attention to everything.
- **Sister Vael** (Healer's Sanctuary): Visits the Refugee Camps south of the walls every other morning to conduct outdoor consultations for those who cannot enter the city. She is always late returning because she stays longer than she planned.
- **Erys Moonwhisper** (Market Quarter): Arrives at the square before dawn. Leaves after the evening announcement. On Vanguard restricted days, she visits the Broken Chalice afterward and does not perform.

## 4.3 Texture Characters — Non-Quest Recurring NPCs

Three characters exist solely to make Hollowford feel like it contains people rather than content nodes. They have no quests. They cannot be interacted with in ways that advance the journal. They are the texture of a city.

**The Singer:**
A Vaelari street performer in the Market Quarter who sings old Dominion songs in a language half the crowd doesn't understand. She performs daily in Act I — she has been performing here since before the Severance and sees no reason to stop. In Act II, after the Ashenveil revelation becomes public, she stops performing. She sits at the edge of the square instead, watching. In Act III, what she does depends on the player's alignment — under Verdant Flame alignment, she begins performing again but the songs are different; under Vanguard alignment, she is gone.

No explanation is ever given for any of these changes. Players who noticed her will feel them.

**The Waiting Man:**
An old Ironkin who sits outside the Garrison every day from morning until the guards change at dusk. He is waiting for news of a grandchild who was stationed outside the Vale when the Severance hit. He is there in Act I. He is there in Act II. He is there in Act III. Whether he receives news — and what kind — is determined by player choices that are not obviously connected to him. The connection becomes clear only in retrospect.

**The Patrol:**
A specific Vanguard soldier who patrols the Market Quarter and has one ambient dialogue line per Act that reflects the world state. In Act I: "Keep moving, nothing to worry about." In Act II: "Eyes open, all of you." In Act III under Vanguard control: "You have a problem? Take it up at the Garrison." Under Reaver alignment: he is not there. Under Verdant Flame alignment: he is there but the line is different — "Lot of strange things happening. Mostly good strange, I think." Under Hollow Crown alignment: he is wearing a House Knight patrol badge over his Vanguard insignia and seems uncertain about it.

## 4.4 District 1 — Market Quarter (Central)

**Faction presence:** Nominally neutral; all factions have agents here.
**Atmosphere:** Loud, overcrowded, desperate, and alive.

**Key Locations:**

*The Broken Chalice Tavern*
The social center of Hollowford. Owner **Breta Hallford** (human, 50s, former caravan organizer) converted the common room into a message board and rumor exchange. She knows everything that moves through Hollowford. She is not a quest giver — she is an information broker who trades gossip for gossip. Players who talk to her regularly receive early warning of several otherwise missable quest hooks.
*Quest hooks:* 3 side quests

*Central Market Square*
Open-air trading, daily and chaotic. **Town Crier Erys Moonwhisper** (Vaelari Driftless, 200s) announces news and faction declarations twice daily. Players who listen to Erys's announcements receive passive world-state updates. Her tone changes between Acts — attentive players will clock the shift before anything else in the city signals it.

*Healer's Sanctuary*
Run by **Sister Vael** (Vaelari Newly Awakened). Overwhelmed, doing extraordinary work. Sells healing consumables and is the entry point for a medical crisis questline. Lady Mirelle's private funding of this clinic is discoverable through observation rather than dialogue.
*Quest hooks:* 2 side quests

*Voss Trading Company*
**Harren Voss** (human, 60s, Merchant Guildmaster). The game's most politically neutral NPC by policy — he pays Vanguard taxes, has a Reaver protection agreement, and sells to Hollow Crown agents at market rate. He mentions Southwatch materials as "theoretically available, if anyone could establish a reliable supply route" — this is a deliberate hook for players who respond to economic framing. It plants Southwatch as a destination without requiring character investment.
*Quest hooks:* 1 side quest

**Act I → Act II first-impression beat:**
Erys Moonwhisper is announcing faction declarations instead of trade news. Her voice is flat in a way it wasn't before. Two Vanguard soldiers stand closer to her than is comfortable. The player who listened to her in Act I will immediately clock that something changed. This beat should be placed so it is the first thing a returning player encounters when re-entering the Market Quarter — Erys's position should be visible from the district entrance.

## 4.5 District 2 — Noble Quarter (Northwest)

**Faction presence:** Hollow Crown (primary); Vanguard (secondary)
**Atmosphere:** Decaying grandeur. Mansions built for a civilization that no longer exists.

**Key Locations:**

*Eltaryn Estate Branch (Hollow Crown Embassy)*
**The Archivist** (name unknown) runs a Hollow Crown intelligence and advocacy operation from a requisitioned townhouse. Primary Hollow Crown quest contact in Act I. Introduction to Seraphine comes through here.
*Quest hooks:* 2 side quests

*Thornwright's Command Post*
Kael Thornwright's operational base. Primary Vanguard quest hub. His personal study (visible at sufficient relationship standing) shows who he actually is: a man from a frontier settlement who has never been comfortable in a place like this. The Command Post contains the garrison's prisoner holding area and armory.
*Quest hooks:* Faction quest hub; 1 side quest

*The Faded Rose*
Exclusive tavern maintaining the fiction that the Dominion still exists. **Lady Mirelle Ashford** (human, 40s, dispossessed noble) holds court here. Mirelle is not evil — she is clinging to the only identity she has ever known. Source of Hollow Crown-adjacent political intelligence.
*Quest hooks:* 1 side quest

## 4.6 District 3 — Vanguard Garrison (Northeast)

**Faction presence:** Ironbound Vanguard (total control)
**Atmosphere:** Military efficiency applied to a civilian space. Cold, organized, functional.

**Key Locations:**

*Garrison Headquarters:* Commander Hale's operational center. Intelligence reports at Trusted standing. Armory at Auxiliary standing.

*Garrison Quartermaster:* Faction merchant. Military gear, rations, supplies.

*The Brig:* Quest-relevant NPCs pass through here. The player can interact with prisoners, break people out, or use standing for interrogation access.
*Quest hooks:* 2 side quests

**Southwatch discoverability hook:**
**Lieutenant Corda** appears in Hollowford once in Act I — she is here for a supply requisition meeting at the Garrison, visibly out of her element in a city environment. She does not offer a quest. She says something in passing that makes a perceptive player want to know her story — something that signals she is carrying something she hasn't resolved. She is gone the next day. Players who ask about her at the Garrison receive a one-line answer: she is posted at Southwatch. This creates character-based pull toward the settlement for players who don't respond to economic framing.

## 4.7 District 4 — Forge District (East)

**Faction presence:** Ironkin craftsmen (cultural dominant); Vanguard (oversight)
**Atmosphere:** Industrial heat, hammering, metal and coal. The most functional part of Hollowford.

**Key Locations:**

*Ironhammer Workshop*
**Torven Ashpeak** (Ironkin, Covenant-aligned). Best source of metal weapons and heavy armor. Torven takes apprentices — player investment in his operation (see Economic Investment, Section 4.9) visibly expands the workshop: a second forge bay, a materials storage addition on the eastern wall, a hand-painted sign replacing the old carved one. The physical change happens within one in-game week of investment. Players who invested can see what their capital did.
*Quest hooks:* 1 side quest

*The Foundry*
Communal forge space. **Sera Flintwick** (human, 30s) manages it. The player's General Workbench is here — accessible to all from Act I. Sera's arrangement with the Reavers (she collects usage data for them in exchange for protection) is discoverable through careful observation and is the entry point for her questline.
*Quest hooks:* 1 side quest

*Refugee Workshops*
Drakeling metalworkers producing unusual alloys. Semi-legal under Vanguard tolerance. Their physical space is visibly improvised — canvas awnings, borrowed tools, temporary everything. If the player invests in protecting the workshops (quest outcome), a permanent structure replaces the improvised setup within two in-game weeks.
*Quest hooks:* 1 side quest

## 4.8 District 5 — Refugee Camps (Outside South Gate)

**Faction presence:** Fragmented. Vanguard processes arrivals; Verdant Flame provides medical assistance; no faction controls this space.
**Atmosphere:** Organized suffering. Managed emergency settlement. Deeply, visibly wrong.

**Notable Areas:**

*Drakeling Sector:* ~400 displaced Drakelings. **Elder Keth** acts as unofficial spokesperson — tired, dignified, with no patience for bureaucracy or charity.

*Vaelari Cluster:* Newly Awakened who gravitated here after emerging near Hollowford. **Lira** (Vaelari, three weeks old) talks to outsiders because the others are afraid to. Aelira, if in party, reacts to this cluster with visible guilt.

*The Transition House:* Breta Hallford's converted warehouse. Player investment visibly expands the building — a second wing, proper beds replacing makeshift pallets, a small cook station outside. The building footprint on the city map changes to reflect expansion.
*Quest hooks:* 2 side quests

**Act II → Act III first-impression beat (camps):**
The camp's entry path has changed. Under Vanguard alignment: a new processing structure is visible from the road before the player arrives — a checkpoint that wasn't there. Under Verdant Flame alignment: a Verdant Flame grove marker has been erected at the camp entrance and a Newly Awakened Vaelari is sitting beneath it in what is clearly meant to be a welcoming posture. Under Reaver alignment: a small impromptu market has appeared just outside the camp perimeter — goods being traded that shouldn't technically be available. Under Hollow Crown alignment: nothing has changed. The same tents. The same lines. A player who remembers what was promised here will feel the absence.

## 4.9 Economic Investment — Visual Requirements

Every economic investment in Hollowford must produce a visible physical change within one in-game week of investment. Players need to *see* that they own something, not merely receive stat bonuses.

**Investment visibility minimums:**
- **Workshop/production investment:** New construction visible on the building (added bay, storage extension, signage change)
- **Property purchase:** Player-specific marker on exterior (subtle — a Voss Trading Company receipt posted in the window, a lock that changed)
- **The Transition House expansion:** Building footprint visibly grows; the city map updates
- **Farm plot investment (Grainfell, Southwatch):** Cleared and planted rows where there was fallow land before
- **Reaver merchant stall (Undercity):** The stall exists with the player's chosen goods on display; a runner NPC periodically brings a ledger

Investment cost structure: Currency (scaled to investment tier) + faction standing minimum (what applies to that station) + quest completion in some cases (earning the right to invest, not just paying). Returns begin after one in-game week and are visible in the economic management screen.

## 4.10 The Undercity — Evolving Underground

### Layer 1 — Criminal Surface (Act I, accessible to all)

The sewer and cellar network beneath the Market Quarter and Forge District. Everyone in Hollowford knows it exists. The Vanguard's attempts to clear it have not held.

**The Broken Grid:** A black market in a large cellar beneath the Market Quarter. Surface-level, accessible without Reaver standing (worse prices, limited selection). Fence services, basic contraband.

**The Drop:** A Reaver-run tavern accessible through a disguised entrance in the Forge District. **"Nail"** evaluates whether the player is worth Varek's time. Passing Nail's assessment (completed missions or dialogue) gates deeper Reaver access.

### Layer 2 — Full Network (Act II, Crew-Adjacent standing)

**The Undercity Market:** Complete black market with full restricted goods inventory. Multiple vendors, private high-value transaction section, Reaver security.

**The Tunnels:** Reaver tunnel network entrance. Maps to non-hub locations fast travel cannot reach.

**Varek's Floor:** Accessible at Trusted Runner standing. Varek's personal quest, the storm engine revelation, and the full intelligence brokerage operation live here.

### Layer 3 — Firstborn Deep (Act II/III, discovery-gated)

Beneath the Reaver network, accessed through a sealed Firstborn door the Reavers themselves haven't opened.

**The Sealed Chamber:** A complete Firstborn monitoring station with the most comprehensive record of the lattice's decline in the Vale. Accessing it requires Hollow Crown archive knowledge OR Relic Hunter origin's Ancient Linguist passive plus significant exploration investment.

The *Below the Floor* side quest plays out here. What the player does with what they find — who they tell, how they use it — is one of the Vale's most consequential freeform decisions.

# SECTION 5: FRONTIER SETTLEMENTS

## 5.1 Ironwood (Northwest Vale)

**Type:** Logging and timber town
**Population:** ~320 post-Severance
**Elevation:** ~180m, dense coniferous forest
**Economic role:** Primary timber. Woodworker's Workshop (specialized station). Wellspring Timber from the Verdantheart-adjacent forest edge.
**Faction presence:** Hollow Crown (active, using logging trails as discreet courier routes); Vanguard (intermittent patrol)

**Key NPCs:**

*Harwick Fell* (human, 50s, logging head and de facto town leader): Cares about Ironwood's survival and nothing else. Honest because he has no political agenda.

*The Hollow Crown Contact (revealed through quest):* One of Ironwood's loggers is a courier. Not a villain — took the work because it paid. What the player does with the discovery has faction consequences.

*Talia Spruce* (human, 20s, highest Woodworking skill in the Vale outside Firstborn ruins): Can be recruited as a specialist worker. Her personal quest must be completed first.

**Quest Lines:**
- *The Logging Problem* (1 quest): Corruption zone encroachment on logging area. Three resolution paths.
- *The Courier* (1 quest): Discovery and resolution of the Hollow Crown contact.
- *Talia's Request* (1 quest): Escort to a Firstborn ruin containing woodworking tools that meaningfully upgrade her output.

**Act II → Act III first-impression beat:**
Entering Ironwood from the southern road, the first visible change under Vanguard alignment is a new checkpoint post where there wasn't one before — a permanent Vanguard structure that clearly wasn't built by the locals. Under Hollow Crown alignment, a House heraldry flag is flying from Harwick Fell's building that was not there before. Harwick is visible through the window. He does not look pleased.

## 5.2 Grainfell (Northeast Vale)

**Type:** Farming village and river crossing
**Population:** ~800 post-Severance (refugee influx)
**Elevation:** ~120m, gentle hills, rich farmland
**Economic role:** Primary food production. Farm plots for player investment. Reagent herbs in the eastern quadrant.
**Faction presence:** Verdant Flame (agricultural research station); Vanguard (food supply monitoring)

**Key NPCs:**

*Aldene Root* (human, 60s, village elder): More administrative knowledge than any NPC in the Vale outside faction leadership. Deeply tired of everyone wanting something from her.

*Brother Fen* (Vaelari, Verdant Flame researcher, 200s): Genuine agricultural scientist developing corruption-resistant crop strains. His work is real, slow, and politically sensitive.

**Verdant Temple pre-framing:**
Before the player enters the Verdant Temple dungeon, Brother Fen (or a Verdant Flame contact at the Hollowford grove camp) tells the player explicitly: "The Rooted One is still trying to do what it was made to do. If you've learned enough about how the Wellspring works, there may be another way." This is framing, not a spoiler — it tells the player the combat resolution is not the only option without revealing what the alternate resolution entails. Players who have the standing and investment will investigate. Players who don't will fight the boss and understand they made a different choice, not that the game withheld information.

**Quest Lines:**
- *The Failing Crop* (1 quest): Corruption affecting fields through a buried lattice node. Three resolution paths.
- *The Distribution Dispute* (1 quest): Vanguard vs. Aldene vs. Brother Fen on grain distribution. Grainfell's food output reflects the outcome for the rest of the game.
- *Fen's Project* (2 quests, sequential): Develop corruption-resistant crops, protect the research, choose who controls the results.
- *Old Road* (1 quest): A Firstborn transit hub buried under the eastern farmland just activated. Hybrid dungeon-investigation.

**Act II → Act III first-impression beat (Verdant Flame aligned):**
Entering Grainfell from the western road, the first thing visible is that the eastern fields — which were wrong in Acts I and II — are a different color. Not dramatically. Just more green than the season warrants. Brother Fen is in the field, not at his research station. He doesn't say anything profound. He's just there, and the field is that color, and that is enough.

## 5.3 Ashford (Northwest Vale, near Highlands border)

**Type:** Mining settlement
**Population:** ~220 post-Severance
**Elevation:** ~240m, higher ground near Hollow Crown Highlands approach
**Economic role:** Iron ore, stone, Uncommon metals. The lower mine level has lattice crystal deposits significant to both major factions present.
**Faction presence:** Hollow Crown (want the lower levels); Vanguard (want everything above)

**Key NPCs:**

*Overseer Brynn Coalhand* (Ironkin, Forge Warden): Runs the mine with military precision. No political allegiance — mines are for mining. Will cooperate with whoever treats him with professional respect.

*Hollow Crown Agent Vesper* (human, 40s, geologist/archivist): Here specifically for the lower mine level. Very good at pretending she is not.

*Named miners (3-4):* A Drakeling with Crimson Flats geological knowledge. A human who was present when the lower level first opened and has said nothing about what he saw. An Ironkin apprentice who is the most reliable Blacksmithing training source outside Hollowford.

**Quest Lines:**
- *The Lower Level* (2 quests): Sealed after two miners didn't return. Investigation, then the decision about who gets the lattice crystal deposits and what Vesper found.
- *The Vanguard Claim* (1 quest): An officer arrives with orders to nationalize mine output. Mediate, support Brynn, support Vanguard, or find a third path.
- *Supply Chain* (1 quest): Corrupted road section between Ashford and Hollowford.

## 5.4 Southwatch (Southeast Vale, Crimson Flats border)

**Type:** Border outpost, refugee processing station
**Population:** ~150 permanent; ~600 in adjacent Drakeling camp
**Elevation:** ~90m, flat southern borderlands
**Economic role:** Heat-resistant metals and drake scales — the only source of these materials in the Vale.
**Faction presence:** Vanguard (official); Drakeling community (adjacent, semi-independent)
**Atmosphere:** The edge of something. The Crimson Flats glow faintly on the southern horizon at night — the Drakeling homeland, still burning.

**Key NPCs:**

*Lieutenant Corda* (human, Vanguard, 28): Young, competent, assigned here as a punishment posting. Doing her job with quiet dignity and growing internal conflict. She appears once in Hollowford in Act I (see Section 4.6 — Garrison Southwatch hook) before the player visits Southwatch. That appearance creates the character-based pull.

*Elder Vorash* (Drakeling, liaison between Emberhaven and Southwatch camps): Focuses here on the most recently arrived refugees — those who just crossed from the Flats, usually in the worst condition.

*Kael in party:* A soldier under his command died in a Crimson Flats expedition six months ago. The soldier's family is in the refugee camp. He hasn't spoken to them. He doesn't know what to say. The player can help him find the words or not.

**Quest Lines:**
- *The Crossing* (1 quest): A group attempting to return to the Flats for family members was caught in a corruption surge. Investigation and recovery.
- *The Soldier's Report* (1 quest): Corda has received orders for harsher refugee processing — biometric recording, mandatory labor for food. She is not going to refuse. But she is going to tell someone.
- *Salvage Run* (2 quests): Accessing Crimson Flats borderland materials. First quest establishes the route; second is the full operation.
- *The Flame and the Oath* (Pyra Flameheart questline, if she is present): A third option beyond stop/join exists for players with sufficient Drakeling standing and Verdant Flame knowledge.

**Act II → Act III first-impression beat (Vanguard aligned):**
A processing checkpoint structure is visible from the southern approach road before the player arrives — new, permanent, clearly not built by the locals. Lieutenant Corda is not at her usual position near the camp entrance. The player has to find her. She is at the back of the checkpoint structure, doing paperwork she was ordered to do. She does not look up immediately when the player approaches.

# SECTION 6: CORRUPTION ZONES

## 6.1 Corruption Zone Design Philosophy

Corruption zones exist on a spectrum: some are traversal hazards (dangerous to cross, not destinations), some are exploration destinations (full areas worth deliberately entering), and all expand or contract based on story progression and player action.

**Universal rules:**
- All zones expand during Act II automatically
- Zone expansion is visible on the world map
- Player actions addressing underlying lattice nodes slow or stop specific zone growth
- Verdant Flame purification can permanently reduce or eliminate zones
- Corruption Herald builds experience zones as empowering rather than hazardous

## 6.2 Vale Corruption Zones

### Zone 1 — The Ashfield Scar (Northeast, near Grainfell)
**Type:** Expansion hazard, active threat to farmland
**Size:** ~0.3 km² (Act I) → ~1.2 km² (Act II if unchecked)
**Enemy composition:** Corrupted wildlife, Corrupted Husks
**Clear condition:** *The Failing Crop* Grainfell quest — stabilizing the buried node stops growth permanently

### Zone 2 — Mistwater Shores (North-central, around Mistwater Lake)
**Type:** Exploration destination
**Size:** ~0.8 km² (stable — predates the Severance, growth already complete)
**Backstory:** A Firstborn water-regulation node beneath the lake failed decades ago during initial lattice degradation. The corruption here is old enough to have developed an ecosystem — creatures that have adapted to it rather than being destroyed by it.
**Enemy composition:** Evolved Corrupted variants, Void Crawlers, the Mistwater Sentinel (named Corrupted bear — boss encounter)
**Clear condition:** Cannot be fully cleared. Verdant Flame reduces passive damage. The node is too deep and too damaged.
**Notable:** One of the Vale's most rewarding exploration zones. A Firstborn data node with significant lore, excellent Shard drops, the Sentinel boss fight.

### Zone 3 — The Broken Road (Southwest, between Hollowford and Ashford)
**Type:** Traversal hazard
**Size:** ~0.2 km² (linear — covers ~800m of road)
**Backstory:** A lattice conduit beneath the western road failed during the Severance. Travel between Hollowford and Ashford now requires fighting through or taking a longer alternate route.
**Clear condition:** Minor quest finds and partially stabilizes the conduit, reducing to a thin hazard strip. Full clearing at Verdant Flame Tier 2 standing.

### Zone 4 — The Sunken Hollow (South-central)
**Type:** Exploration destination with dungeon entrance
**Size:** ~0.6 km²
**Backstory:** A circular depression always avoided by local farmers — "the hollow where nothing grows right." The Severance revealed why: a sealed Firstborn vault beneath it had been leaking for centuries. Now accessible through a sinkhole.
**Enemy composition:** Corrupted wildlife (dense), Lattice Wraiths, Undead (Dominion soldiers caught in the surge)
**Clear condition:** Completing the Sunken Hollow dungeon stops zone expansion. Partial purification at Verdant Flame Tier 3.

### Zone 5 — The Corruption Front (Eastern Vale, spreading from Verdantheart)
**Type:** Dynamic expansion zone — the largest and most story-relevant in the Vale
**Size:** ~1.5 km² (Act I) → ~4 km² (Act II if unchecked)
**Backstory:** The primary corruption incursion from the Wellspring destabilization in Verdantheart. Predates the Severance. The Severance accelerated it dramatically.
**Enemy composition:** Full spectrum, Act II/III difficulty at eastern edge. Contains ruins of three abandoned Vale farmsteads now serving as corrupted dungeon-lite environments.
**Clear condition:** Addressing Wellspring events in Verdantheart main story (Act II) significantly slows growth. Cannot be cleared without Verdantheart main quest completion.

# SECTION 7: WILDERNESS AREAS AND POINTS OF INTEREST

## 7.1 Named Landmarks (Selected)

**Sunspear Ridge (Eastern hills):**
Overlooks Verdantheart. The Vale's most visually impressive non-dungeon location — corruption front's purple-black edge, Hollowford's smoke, Verdantheart's canopy, all visible simultaneously. A Vaelari NPC watches Verdantheart here daily. With Vaelari race or significant Verdant Flame standing, she eventually explains why.

**Blackstone Quarry (Northwest):**
An abandoned mining operation that hit a pocket of raw Void Ore in a lattice-adjacent formation. Shut down after workers showed symptoms. The workers are still there. Medium-density combat zone with excellent Void Ore deposits and a small Firstborn sub-structure.

**The Old Ford (South of Hollowford):**
Where the River Serpentis was crossed before Hollowford's bridge was built. The Severance has made the water behave strangely: it flows in both directions simultaneously for approximately 200m.

**Discoverable resolution:** The Old Ford sits above a lattice junction where two conduits crossed. When the Severance fractured both simultaneously, the energy signatures locked into a self-sustaining feedback loop. Before the Ashenveil Archive, this is inexplicable. After the Archive, a player who returns and uses Echo Interface sees the junction clearly for the first time — and can choose to collapse it (river normalizes, minor loot reward) or leave it (the anomaly persists as a world landmark; the Verdant Flame considers it significant and explains why at Circle Ally standing). Two outcomes, both discoverable without a formal quest. The mystery has an answer. Players who find it feel rewarded rather than misled.

**The Remembrance Field (Central Vale):**
A large open meadow where Dominion soldiers were buried after a civil conflict 60 years ago. Four thousand graves. Since the Severance, the field produces Undead at night — not all of them, not consistently. The Vanguard is suppressing information about this because the public morale implications are severe.

# SECTION 8: DUNGEONS

## 8.1 The Sepulcher (Southwest, Act I Finale)
**Level range:** 15–30 | **Size:** 3 levels, ~75 minutes full
**Identity:** The Firstborn at their most functional — a sealed preservation vault whose systems are active and whose constructs are doing their jobs without updated threat definitions.
**Boss:** The Vault Keeper (Warden Construct, 3-phase fight including the room itself as a weapon in Phase 2)
**Narrative payoff:** Partial record of Erevos's last communications before the Consciousness was activated. The Echo reacts more strongly here than anywhere previously visited.
**Loot:** Anchor Shards, Forge Shards, The Warden's Argument Remnant, one Sentinel's Array set piece

## 8.2 Verdant Temple (Northeast, Act II Minor)
**Level range:** 25–40 | **Size:** 2 levels, ~30 minutes
**Identity:** Vaelari sacred space predating the Dominion, built over a natural Wellspring spring that the lattice was later routed through. The natural and engineered energies are fighting each other here.
**Boss:** The Rooted One — a Corrupted ancient Grove Sentinel. Combat resolution and purification alternate path (Circle Ally standing + Nature Magic investment). Players are told about the alternate path by Brother Fen before entering (see Section 5.2). Not a retroactive surprise — a forward-framed choice.
**Narrative payoff:** Evidence that the Wellspring and the Firstborn lattice were not designed to coexist. Aelira's most significant character moment.
**Loot:** Verdant Shards, Ward Shards, Grove Mantle Bound set pieces, Fracture Walker Remnant (combat path only — alternate resolution produces different rewards)

## 8.3 Sunken Hollow Vault (South-central, Act II)
**Level range:** 30–50 | **Size:** 4 levels, ~90 minutes full
**Identity:** A Firstborn vault sealed to contain a prototype weapon system that was never deployed. The Severance fractured it and the weapon is destabilizing.
**Boss:** The Architect's Echo — a residual consciousness impression left by the Firstborn engineer who sealed this vault. A Lattice Titan variant that speaks Firstborn fragments during the encounter. The player's Echo responds strongly.
**Narrative payoff:** The weapon system is directly relevant to Act III. Players who cleared this dungeon understand why one ending option carries the weight it does. Players who didn't can still complete that ending but with less context.
**Loot:** Void Shards, Fracture Shards, Corruption Herald gear, one Erevos Fragment set piece, The Last Record Remnant staff

## 8.4 Ashenveil Archive (Northeast Mountains, Act II Major)
**Level range:** 40–65 | **Size:** 7 levels, ~5 hours full
**Identity:** The Firstborn's primary knowledge archive for the Vale region — a continental library cut into the mountainside with complete records of the lattice's construction, operation, and planned decommissioning. The most important location in Act II.
**Boss:** The Architect's Hand — apex Construct, multiple phases, all combat systems required
**Narrative payoff:** The Consuming Consciousness origin story. Erevos's design philosophy. The original decommissioning plan vs. the corrupted execution. This revelation triggers the Act II world state transition for all factions.
**Loot:** Archive Shards (highest concentration), remaining Erevos Fragment set pieces, Sentinel's Array pieces, Custom Remnant crafting access (Firstborn Forge unlocks here)

# SECTION 9: THE VALE ACROSS ACTS

## 9.1 Act I — Crisis

Default world state. Player learning the world. Corruption zones small and new. Undercity Layer 1 only. Refugee camps growing but stable.

**Act I automatic world changes (story milestone triggers):**
- After Sepulcher completion: Vanguard patrol intensity near ruins increases. Small change, visible to attentive players.
- After first faction quest: That faction's Hollowford presence becomes slightly more visible. A new contact NPC appears near their headquarters.

## 9.2 Act II — Conflict

Ashenveil revelation triggers Act II. Each faction interprets it according to their ideology.

**Automatic Act II changes:**
- Hollowford: Faction tension visible in all districts. New faction NPCs with Act II quests.
- Corruption Front (Zone 5): Significant expansion. Eastern Vale noticeably more dangerous.
- Vanguard checkpoints: Two new checkpoints on roads between Hollowford and frontier settlements.
- Undercity: Layer 2 accessible to players with Crew-Adjacent standing.
- Drakeling camps: Pyra Flameheart's following grows.

**Player-driven Act II deviations (examples):**
- *The Failing Crop* resolved: Grainfell's eastern fields show recovered color. Zone 1 visibly smaller.
- *Distribution Dispute* resolved in Aldene's favor: Refugee camp food lines more stable.
- Ironwood's Hollow Crown contact exposed to Vanguard: Hollow Crown agents absent from Ironwood; new Vanguard surveillance visible.
- Transition House invested in: Building visibly expanded on the city map.

## 9.3 Act III — Resolution

Act III alignment reshapes the Vale's default state. Each alignment has a distinct world identity with genuine trade-offs.

---

### VANGUARD ALIGNMENT — The Controlled Vale

*"Someone has to maintain order."*

**Hollowford:** Checkpoints at all district entrances. Noble Quarter mansions fully requisitioned. Market Quarter under price controls. Vanguard heraldry on public buildings. Erys Moonwhisper's announcements are now officially sanctioned statements — she reads from a prepared text.

**Frontier settlements:** All four have formal Vanguard garrison posts. Roads fully maintained.

**Corruption zones:** Vanguard suppression operations active — not clearing zones but containing them through military force. The zones are stable but not smaller.

**Character:** The Vale is measurably safer. It is also more controlled in ways some NPCs find worse than the alternative. The Waiting Man outside the Garrison has received news. The Patrol soldier's line has changed to something that sounds like policy.

---

### REAVER ALIGNMENT — The Open Vale

*"Power belongs to whoever takes it."*

**Hollowford:** Undercity Layer 2 partially visible to surface level. Black market goods appear in the Market Quarter without concealment. Noble Quarter has several properties that have quietly changed hands through shell transactions. The Singer is performing again but the songs are different — older, rougher, Reaver-adjacent.

**Frontier settlements:** All four have Reaver-adjacent merchant presence. Smuggling routes bypass all Vanguard checkpoints.

**Corruption zones:** Reavers are actively harvesting corruption materials. Zones are expanding faster because nobody is managing them — but the player's access to corruption materials significantly improves.

**Character:** The Vale is economically active in ways it wasn't. It is also more dangerous in ways that some NPCs find better than the alternative. The old Ironkin waiting outside the Garrison is gone — news arrived through a Reaver intelligence channel, not an official one.

---

### VERDANT FLAME ALIGNMENT — The Healing Vale

*"Let it end gracefully."*

**Hollowford:** Verdant Flame openly present in all districts. Corruption zone edges near the city have visibly receded — golden-green light at zone boundaries is visible from the walls at dawn.

**Frontier settlements:** Grainfell's corruption-resistant crops distributed. Ironwood's northern edge shows active Verdant Flame grove markers. Brother Fen is in the Grainfell fields.

**Corruption zones:** All zones show measurable reduction. Zone 1 (Ashfield Scar) may be fully cleared if Act II Grainfell quests were completed.

**Character:** The Vale is recovering in ways that feel natural. It is slower to develop politically — the Verdant Flame's focus on the land means less attention to the human institutions that are also failing. The Vaelari street performer has returned to the Market Quarter square. The songs are different from both what she sang in Act I and what she might have sung in Reaver alignment. They are new.

---

### HOLLOW CROWN ALIGNMENT — The Known Vale

*"Information is the only power that survives catastrophe."*

The Hollow Crown cannot fight corruption. They cannot purify land. They cannot establish military order. What they can do is understand the system well enough to predict where it fails — and act on that knowledge before the failure happens.

**The Boundary System:**
Using the combined knowledge from the Ashenveil Archive, the Undercity's Firstborn Deep, and their own archive holdings, the Hollow Crown has identified the specific lattice nodes driving each corruption zone's expansion. They cannot stop the corruption. But they can predict exactly where it will spread next.

The visible Act III change is not zones shrinking — it is zone edges being *marked*. Hollow Crown surveyors and House Knights have established perimeter lines using Firstborn cartographic techniques. The corruption inside the line is abandoned and formally accepted as lost. Everything outside the line is being organized, protected, and prepared for the long-term recovery that the other alignments cannot quite plan for because they're too busy responding.

**Hollowford:** Eltaryn heraldry on Noble Quarter buildings. Vanguard presence reduced there, replaced by House Knights. The Market Quarter has noble luxury goods reappearing in stalls that were selling survival rations six months ago.

**Frontier settlements:** Ashford's mine under Hollow Crown administration — the lower level's lattice crystals being systematically extracted and catalogued. Ironwood's Hollow Crown contact now operating openly.

**Corruption zones:** Zone edges marked with survey stakes bearing Eltaryn seals. Zone interiors abandoned as formally written-off territory. Everything outside the lines: maintained, documented, governed.

**Character:** The Vale is smaller than it was. What remains is stable, governed, and positioned for eventual recovery in a way the other alignments aren't. Players who chose this alignment feel like they made a hard, adult decision — they accepted losses that the Verdant Flame might have prevented, in exchange for stability that the Vanguard can't quite achieve and legitimacy that the Reavers can't provide. The town crier Erys Moonwhisper is now reading House Eltaryn official announcements. She has more lines than she did under the Vanguard. She sounds like she has an opinion about them.
